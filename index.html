The interesting gap

ZK that does real work — not a sticker

Stellar shipped the cryptographic primitives. ShieldPay turns them into a finished, real-world payment flow. Remove the proof, and the system literally cannot validate a transfer.

1 · Proof in your browser

A Circom circuit proves a valid shielded transfer (inputs = outputs, ownership, Merkle membership) client-side via WASM. Secrets never leave your device.

2 · Verified on Stellar

A Soroban Groth16 verifier checks the proof on-chain using the BN254 + Poseidon host functions. The amount stays hidden; validity becomes public.

3 · Selective disclosure

An auditor holding a view key can reconstruct the amount and counterparty — privacy for the public, accountability for regulators.

\uD83D\uDE1F The problem

  • Public ledgers expose every salary and every B2B payment amount.
  • Full anonymity breaks KYC/AML — regulators say no.
  • Institutions can adopt neither extreme, so real money stays off-chain.

✨ The ShieldPay way

  • Private by default — amounts & parties shielded with ZK.
  • Compliant by design — view-key disclosure + ASP allow-list.
  • Real rails — USDC stablecoin transfers on Stellar.

4
