The interesting gap
ZK that does real work — not a sticker

Stellar shipped the cryptographic primitives. ShieldPay turns them into a finished, real-world payment flow. Remove the proof and the system literally can't validate a transfer.

🧩

1 · Proof in your browser

A Circom circuit proves a valid shielded transfer (inputs = outputs, ownership, Merkle membership) client-side via WASM. Secrets never leave your device.

⛓️

2 · Verified on Stellar

A Soroban Groth16 verifier checks the proof on-chain using BN254 + Poseidon host functions. The amount stays hidden; validity becomes public.

🔑

3 · Selective disclosure

An auditor holding a view key can reconstruct amount and counterparty — privacy for the public, accountability for regulators.

😟 The problem

  • ✕ Public ledgers leak every salary and B2B payment amount.
  • ✕ Full anonymity breaks KYC/AML — regulators say no.
  • ✕ Institutions can adopt neither extreme, so real money stays off-chain.

✨ The ShieldPay way

  • ✓ Private by default — amounts & parties shielded with ZK.
  • ✓ Compliant by design — view-key disclosure + ASP allow-list.
  • ✓ Real rails — USDC stablecoin transfers on Stellar.
End-to-end flow

Every step is real: proofs are generated client-side and verified by a Soroban contract on testnet.

1

2

Prove

Generate a Groth16 proof of a valid transfer in the browser.

3

Verify

Soroban verifier checks the proof; ASP allow-list is enforced.

4

Disclose

Auditor reconstructs details with the view key when required.
